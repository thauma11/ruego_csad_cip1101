Overcoming Adversities

John Martin Ruego

Childhood

        Growing up, my childhood was marked by frequent moves and a somewhat blurry early memory.1 Despite the challenges I faced, I was able to persevere and find the courage to stand up for myself.2 I was born in Manila where I spent my first two years of school at Cecilio Apostol Elementary School, where I developed a love for learning and knowledge.3 However, when I was in 2nd grade, my parents went through a difficult separation and we had to move to Quezon City.4 I continued my education at Tandang Sora Elementary School, where I spent grades 3 to 6.5

        It was during this time that I faced physical bullying from some of my classmates, which was a difficult and trying experience for me.6 I had to learn how to stand up for myself, to be strong in the face of adversity.7 Despite these challenges, I refused to let the bullies get the best of me and instead channeled my energy into my studies and new interests.8 In 5th grade, I discovered my love for music and started playing the guitar.9 This newfound passion for music became an outlet for me to express myself and a way to find solace during difficult times.10 I was also an avid player of Legos and had a passion for computer games, which were my ways of coping with difficult situations.11

        My determination to do well in school and to show my bullies that I was more than just a target for their ridicule paid off when I graduated with honors in elementary school, showcasing my strength and resilience.12 My childhood experiences taught me the value of hard work, perseverance, and determination, and these are lessons that have stayed with me all throughout my growing up years.13 I have learned that it's not about avoiding challenges, but about how we choose to face them.14 By focusing on our passions and interests, we can find the courage to overcome any obstacle.15 These valuable lessons have shaped who I am today and have allowed me to navigate life's challenges with confidence and grace.16

        Throughout my life, I have also learned to value education and knowledge. I believe that education is the key to success and to living a fulfilling life.17 I have taken this belief to heart and have made it my mission to continue learning and growing in all areas of my life.18 I believe that by doing so, I will be better equipped to face life's challenges and to make a positive impact in the world.19 And this is something that I will always hold dear and carry with me throughout my journey.20

Teenage

        In my teenage life, I experienced a rollercoaster of emotions and adventures.1 My high school journey began at Tandang Sora National High School, where I formed a band with some of my classmates and honed my musical skills.2 Our band was passionate about music, and we spent countless hours practicing and writing new material.3 Our hard work paid off when we were selected to play in various battles of the bands and gigs, providing us with opportunities to showcase our talent to a broader audience.4

        However, my life took an unexpected turn when my mother had to fly abroad for work, leaving me and my siblings in the care of our grandfather.5 Despite this challenge, I remained focused on my studies and continued to make the best out of my high school years.6 I was determined not to let the situation impact my future, and I threw myself into my studies and my music.7

        In grade 9, I made the difficult decision to continue my studies and leave the band.8 I knew that I couldn’t take on both commitments, and I didn’t want to stop my studies.9 I was passionate about music, but I was also passionate about getting the best education possible.10 My mother’s support made it possible for me to pursue both my education and my passion.11

        Thanks to my mother's support, I was able to enter a prestigious private school, Philippine Women's University Jose Abad Santos Memorial School, for my senior high school years.12 This provided me with an exceptional education and numerous opportunities to grow and develop as a person.13 I was surrounded by talented and driven students, and I was constantly inspired to push myself further.14

        During grade 11, I had the chance to showcase my musical talent one last time at a school event, which was a proud moment for me.15 I performed in front of my classmates and teachers, and the experience was both exhilarating and a bit nerve-wracking.16 But I was proud of myself for facing my fears and putting myself out there.17

        Despite the challenges posed by the pandemic, I remained dedicated to my studies and continued to pursue my passion for music by writing and composing songs in my free time.18 I was grateful for the time to reflect and explore my creativity, and I was proud of the songs I created.19 I am proud to say that I graduated with honors from my senior high school, a testament to my hard work and determination.20

        In conclusion, my teenage years were a time of growth and self-discovery, filled with challenges and opportunities.21 I am grateful for the experiences and lessons I've learned and proud of the person I have become.22 My high school years will always hold a special place in my heart, as a time of friendship, music, and personal growth. I will never forget the moments I shared with my bandmates, the friends I made, and the teachers who inspired me.23 These experiences have shaped who I am today and will stay with me for the rest of my life.24

College

        After successfully completing my senior high school with honors, I was faced with a new challenge: choosing my college program.1 Despite my passion for music, I was hesitant to pursue it as a career due to practical considerations, as I had heard from my mother and other relatives that there wasn't much money in the industry.2 However, my background in ICT from senior high school led me to enroll in a related field, Computer Science, at the University of the East Manila.3

        The first year of college was unique in that it was held entirely online due to the ongoing pandemic.4 While this mode of learning had its benefits, such as not having to leave the house and having the flexibility to set my own pace, it also posed some challenges.5 Studying on my own was not as easy as I thought, and I found myself struggling to stay motivated and engaged in my coursework.6

        The second year of college was a mixed experience, with the second semester being held in a hybrid format that combined online and in-person classes.7 Although I found the in-person classes to be more engaging and interactive, the daily commute to and from school was tiring and took a toll on my energy levels.8

        In conclusion, my college years have been a mix of challenges and rewards.9 Despite the difficulties, I am grateful for the opportunity to pursue higher education and build a solid foundation for my future.10 I am confident that with hard work and determination, I will be able to achieve my career goals and make a positive impact on the world.11 In addition to my coursework, I have been thinking about joining in some extra-curricular activities, such as joining the some of the university's club like the UE Jam or the COMSSO.12 This could allow me to develop new skills, network with others in the field.13

        Although my journey so far has not been without its challenges, I am proud of what I have accomplished and excited for what the future holds.14 I have learned so much about the field of computer science and how it can be used to make a positive impact on society.15 I have also developed strong relationships with my classmates, who have provided me with valuable guidance and support along the way.16

        Looking on my college journey so far, I am amazed at how much I have grown and how much I have yet to learn.17 I am determined to continue to work hard, seek out new experiences, and make the most of my time in college.18 I believe that by remaining focused, dedicated, and persistent, I will be able to achieve my long-term goals and make a meaningful contribution to the world.19. As I enter my second year of college, I am filled with a sense of excitement and determination.20 I am eager to continue my education, learn new things, and face new challenges.20 I am confident that with the skills and knowledge I have gained so far, I will be well-prepared to succeed in the years ahead.21
